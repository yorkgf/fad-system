学生背景
小杨来自成都，文学、艺术、表演、运动，样样都行。这位同学身上有一股倔劲儿，认定的事情就一定努力到底。这比较适合芝加哥大学的学风。小杨有过从事股票和期货的经历。她还去芝加哥参加过美洲文化研究的会议，在这次会议上见到了很多校友，还代表学校团体做了演讲。这次活动对她影响很大，因为她得到了很多老师的鼓励。小杨选的题目是一首诗：“生活和梦想之间有一个第三。”就这句话写一段文章。我们在设计文书时，将其设计成一封信，写给这位诗人。生活和梦想之间存在的是“可能性”。

Question 1. How does the University of Chicago, as you know it now, satisfy your desire for a particular kind of learning, community, and future? Please address with some specificity your own wishes and how they relate to UChicago.
问题1（必答）：芝加哥大学如何满足你学习生活和未来打算的需求？请具体说明你的需求并将它们与芝加哥大学联系起来。
分析
写作前，好好考虑一下为什么你要进芝加哥大学。如果你的回答是“It's a great school" or "It has a great reputation”它是一个好学校或它有一个好名声，那么芝加哥大学就一定不适合你。你可以说什么？可以说，“在所有的大学中，芝加哥大学的老师和学生是第一个告诉我它跟其他学校不一样的！”这才是真正的匹配。
多数学校都会问“为什么你要来这里？”其实学校想听的不是他们有什么，而是你的故事。比如说，你讲他们有诺贝尔获奖者、有精品班级、有美丽的校园……这些都是他们知道的。所以你的答案必须包括为什么你认为这个学校对你有帮助，能对你的成功起到推波助澜的作用。这样的答案才是录取委员会的人想听的。
范文
在我拜访芝加哥大学期间，我被大学的跨学科教育所吸引——在49个领域里面，我可以随意挑选课程来学习。跨专业学习教给我们创造性和新视角以及不同寻常的见解，甚至是怪异的方法。
Shakespeare and Plato, Einstein and Picasso, quantum physics and Brahms, I believe all of these are well balanced at this great campus of Chicago. I choose Chicago simply because I identify with its liberal education concepts of cross-boundary studies, and I believe firmly that Chicago can help me exceed my personal potential so that I can evolve into a leader who can make changes happen. During my trip to University of Chicago this August, I was enchanted by its interdisciplinary undergraduate education. Chicago maintains a community where each student feels a sense of belonging. Unlike many other colleges that clearly separate the turfs between graduate and undergrad students, Chicago has a great advantage that we undergrads can readily draw upon resources from all the graduate campus and all professional schools. We can surf, explore and test-fly courses or learning in any of the 49 areas of study, which will make me knowledgeable in terms of interdisciplinary views and comparative methods towards my own study. Chicago educates us in both ways, core learning giving us critical thinking, analysis, good concise writing and translatable skills while cross-boundary learning imparting in us creativeness, new perspectives, unusual insights, or even bizarre approaches.
独特的思维模式来源于充满活力的学习氛围，我相信芝加哥大学就是这样一个地方。不仅如此，芝加哥大学的教授们对于我来说是构思巧妙的、鼓舞人心的、特立独行的先行者。我喜欢芝加哥大学，除了它的跨学科教育，还在于它丰富的社区志愿活动以及高强度的课程系统。
Yes, bizarre or unconventional thinking, which goes far beyond existing boundaries, must come from an organic and vibrant community of learning. I believe Chicago is such a place, where most of undergraduate classes are taught by real energetic professors—people who believe in their undergraduates and are willing to invest as much in them as they do for postgraduates. Nobel Prize winners or not, they are to me ingenious, inspiring and maybe maverick forerunners. I will choose more, and I will experience abundantly, as here in Chicago each student is seen as completely individual and allows him/her the autonomy to form our own curriculum, no matter how bizarre it is. Furthermore, as a boy who is very enthusiastic in school and volunteer activities, I was also attracted by its University Community Service Center(UCSC),which fosters the development of civic-minded students by providing substantive community service opportunities. That links me better to Chicago City as the realistic world beyond the textbook figures or charts. University of Chicago encouraged students to engage in researches across multiple disciplines, which is generally reserve exclusively for graduate students. In college, I want to get my hands wet and my feet busy doing all sorts of different things, regardless of the tag on it saying "should" or "should not." Lastly, do I like the intensive course system at Chicago? A resounding "Yes”! I really enjoy this kind of setting because I have more flexibility when I choose my courses. Such a fast tempo can also spur me on because there will be frequent exams and essays, so this way I can keep myself geared up all the time and build a solid foundation for the future. The interdisciplinary education, the colorful community service, together with other unconventional but really adorable features or resources in Chicago, all make me believe Chicago is home to my dream and desire.
分析
作者从四个方面讲述了选择芝加哥大学的原因。有跨专业的自由教育理念，对“我”来讲可以选择不同的专业来学习；充满活力的学习氛围以及博学的教授，“我”可以掌握独特的思维模式；实质性的社区服务机会，让“我”在学习之外能接触社会；密集型的课程体系，鞭策“我”不断向上。每一个理由作者都是以自己为中心，讲述自己的故事，说明芝加哥大学是如何跟自己匹配，这就是作者的高明之处，也是这篇文章称得上优秀的地方。可以看出，这些理由都是作者去实地考察、网络搜索后得到的答案。如果没有这番功夫，怎么能知道芝加哥大学的interdisciplinary undergraduate education?怎么能知道fly courses or learning in any of the 49 areas of study?怎么能知道University Community Service Center (UCSC)?又怎么能知道the intensive course system at Chicago?

范文
利奥·梅拉梅德（被誉为“现代期货市场之父”）的《向传统呐喊：全球最大期货交易所的创新与转型》是我爱读的书。
"For Crying Out Loud" written by Leo Melamed is my favorite book. Mr. Melamed, the former Chairman and currently the Chairman Emeritus of Chicago Mercantile Exchange (CME Group), is well known as the father of modern futures markets and the founder of financial futures. He is the person who changed the CME fundamentally and made it the success as it is today.
在书中，梅拉梅德不仅讲述了几十年来期货市场从商品期货到金融期货、从公开喊价到电子化交易的革命性发展，还细致地描述了金融变革中的种种交锋、代表人物和策略。作者还把与金融市场交织在一起的技术性事件和全球性事件一起叙述，以便读者更好地理解历史背景。
In the book Mr. Melamed told the story about the revolutionary development of the futures markets, the difficulties / barriers they had during this period, and how they promote new ideas, thoughts and technologies which are the momentums of this development. He also described the battles and strategies that transformed the futures markets from open outcry (live animal trading in 1960s) to the electronic screen, the one we have today worldwide. The book discussed the values of the hedging facility, the benefit we can have by managing almost all risks and reducing exposures, these all come from modern futures exchanges.
今年8月，我有幸在芝加哥大学亲眼得见梅拉梅德先生，我深受先生的鼓舞和激励，并且也为他的人格魅力而吸引。我开始不断掌握国内外事件，在统计、数学和商业领域进行更广泛的阅读。
I have read the book three times, knowing almost in and out of those charts, figures and stories. Which was more exciting, during my trip to the States this August, I was lucky to have the honor to meet Mr. Melamed in person at campus of University of Chicago, where he delivered the speech to us, briefing the development of futures exchanges in this dynamic world and how they have and will continue to impact and change people's life. I was influenced and inspired not only by the story of the remarkable life and career in his book but also by the intelligence and unique intriguing personality he showed us. My affinity to the book and its author has a personal factor. Due to my passion in business and desire to cultivate finance consciousness, I was engaged in stock transactions for a period of time with the support of my mother. I put my gift money in the stock account. My confidence grew when I saw how people earned from the stock market. Since I knew little on stock investment, I found it hard to decide the stocks that I could settle on and invest on them. As a result I learnt that in business it all depended on the marketing environment and making or not making profits. From the experiences I got during this time, I deeply felt that financial knowledge cannot be learned in a short term. It has to be like what Mr. Melamed had told me, "a systematic change." Then, I gave up stocks, accumulated financial knowledge with goals, kept myself abreast of domestic and international events and read more extensively on statistics, math and business subjects. During the following years I always have the interest and desire to find out the secrets behind the ups and downs, twists and turns, puts or calls of the market. It is indeed fascinating and a lot more complicated than I thought it to be.

《向传统呐喊：全球最大期货交易所的创新与转型》，如果不是对经济学感兴趣，应该没人想读这本书。文章第一段介绍这本书的作者利奥·梅拉梅德，第二段介绍这本书的大体内容，而第三段，也是最重要的段落，则讲述作者为什么喜欢这本书以及梅拉梅德。

范文（for Essay Option 3）
Dear Mr. Antonio Machado,
我挣扎了很长时间，试图猜测生活和梦想之间的第三件事。我思索了好多，从爱到精神、到希望、到时间、到创新、到智力、到刻苦学习以及更多。我们不能将生活与梦想同自己分开。我们自己就是生活和梦想中间的第三件事。
I struggled for a long time trying to guess the third thing between living and dreaming. I have many speculations—from love, to spirituality, to hope, to time, to creativity, to intelligence, to hard working, and more. In the end, I realized that all these are either some traits of ourselves or some actions we have to carry out. We cannot separate living and dreaming from ourselves. We ourselves are the third thing between living and dreaming.
作者想到了卡夫卡的成名作《变形记》，书中的格里高尔一夜之间变成了一只大甲虫，且不被旁人甚至是家人接受。
I would say I see Kafka in you and your lines. It reminded me of Gregor in Metamorphosis, the sad story about how he woke up one morning and found himself transformed into a gigantic beetle not accepted by his family and others, even got apples thrown on him by his father.
作者认为自己就像那只大甲虫一般，因为思想跟常人有很大的不同。接下来讲述自己在英国学习时的一些所感与所得。
Many times I feel I am that beetle that has so vastly different thoughts from most of my peers. If Gregor has suffered terminal injury by the fallen apple in Metamorphosis after he changed, I however survived, dodging agilely and sometimes bracing up to withstand those unexpected poundings. Guess what, I can guess myself and myself cannot even form a valid prediction of what is to come! More often than not, I guess that if I could pause and guess for myself more frequently, the carapace（甲壳）I carry may not have been so heavy, or prone to break. It has now some visible cracks on the surface now, I can tell. We live in a world where the ultimate expression of our existence on this planet is to be busy, very busy indeed, producing, studying and struggling every minute. Few actually believe that living, dreaming and ourselves are actually the same thing in varied forms. After graduate from elementary school, I really wanted to go abroad to experience the outside world. I went to England on my own at age 12 (with a group of other older students) in spite of the oppositions from my family. There I saw things I've never experienced before, and I met some people who were living their dreams in life. At then I met that Australian guitar boy singing near a bridge at Oxford who told me he traveled alone across continents for the past 2 years. "What was your dream and why?" my innocent but stupid question seemed misplaced. "Nothing, just be myself, and tell me, what do you mean by dream?" I couldn't answer that. For many times later, I can only guess my answers to questions like this. But my experiences indeed have added another layer of carapace to my back, protecting me from those falling apples.
跟格里高尔不同的是，我能有勇气去做我自己并去改变世界。
In my opinion, Gregor should not have overly worried for his appearance, for that might well be just one flash of his dreamt life or he was living one of his dream. Can you, Mr. Machado, so easily face the inquisition? The moment when you start to guess, you have already become one. I don't want to be a beetle like Gregor hiding in the corner of a room admitting his fate of being a beetle. I believe in myself that I have the courage and ability to be myself and make some difference in this world. The Literature Society in school published nothing but rubbish (do forgive my rashness again) and the magazine became only a vehicle for students aspiring to score credits satisfying college entrance requirements. I was elected president of the Society at my 10th grade, but wanted to change its ethos and its philosophy once and for all. But when I first announced my plan that themes of magazine should be open to all, funding should be more actively pursued among city business, selection of articles should be more liberal, it was opposed by almost everyone.
我和几位核心队员坚持并取得了成功。我想无论生活和梦想之间有什么选择，最好的办法就是向前进并大声喊出你的名字。
I, together with a few core team members, persisted and succeed in the end. Those days and nights were intense, but affirming in me there is no guess between the falling apples and your shell. That is only myself. Do it, or give up and live/dream/ guess something else. In fact every person might be a beetle in eyes of others because the beetle always has his own dream and pursue not understood by others. There is no such corner to hide in the room. I think no matter whatever choice is between living and dreaming, stepping forward to say out your name loudly is always the best thing to do.
我不仅想要成为我自己，而且想改变生活。我想告诉大家，我们可以做很多，而不仅仅是在生活与梦想间挣扎，我们可以为自己而活，并且去追求自己的梦想。
I'm fortunate to have my family unlike Gregor's. They never rejected me even they might oppose to my decisions at first. Now I don't just want to be myself but also want to make a difference in life. I want others to know that we can do more than merely struggling between living and dreaming—we can stand up for ourselves and live our own dreams. I hope University of Chicago can be my new family and hold me to stand up taller and see further.
Mr. Machado, I and we are the third thing in between living and dreaming. I am carrying the carapace and the apples rain on it. I know you would never see this letter and I am not sure whether my guesses would satisfy you. Why don't I just hand this letter over and let UChicago judge for my guesses.
Sincerely yours,
Xxx

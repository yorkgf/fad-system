四　Massachusetts Institute of Technology 麻省理工学院
学生背景
小林来自深圳，父母都是做生意的，对留学不很了解。小林很小的时候就表现出与众不同的才能。仅凭他全球奥数第四的数学才能，就足以到麻省理工学院留学。所以文书对此做一个恰如其分地表现就可以。他分数不是特别高，SAT1900多，托福110多分，但申请不到十五天就被录取。
案例
题目
Rather than asking you to write one long essay, the MIT application consists of several short response questions and essays designed to help us get to know you. Remember that this is not a writing test. These are the places in the application where we look for your voice—who you are, what drives you, what's important to you, what makes you tick. Be honest, be open, be authentic—this is your opportunity to connect with us.
我们不要求你写一篇长文，我们希望你能回答一些问题并写几篇短文，以便于我们更好地了解你。请记住，这不是作文测试。我们只是想听到你的声音——你是谁，你的梦想，为什么选择MIT。诚实些，大胆些，真实些——这是我们想要的。
You should certainly be thoughtful about your essays, but if you're thinking too much—spending a lot of time stressing or strategizing about what makes you "look best”, as opposed to the answers that are honest and easy—you're doing it wrong.
思考你的写作内容。但如果你期盼写出让你表面上好看的文章而去花费大量的时间谋划思索，你就做错了！这不是我们希望看到的诚实和简单。

•成为一个优秀的人
Describe your most important academic accomplishment or intellectual experience to date. We don't want to know about test scores or course grades, rather we want to know about your creativity, your willingness to take intellectual risks or your affinity for scholarly endeavors.
描述你迄今为止最重要的学术成果或经验。考试分数或课程成绩不是我们所关注的重点。相反，我们看中你的创造力、学术上冒风险的意愿以及对学术的喜好。
分析
最重要的学术成果，可写的很多。包括参加的一次比赛、写过的报告、出过的一本书或者是有过的小发明、新发现。内容不重要，重要的是一定要在里面展示出你的热情以及你所具备的创造能力创新能力。在写的时候，一定要确保描述清楚、细致到位。很多同学拿到这个话题就忙不迭地列举一系列的成就，这样对吗？其实不然，将一两个事例讲清楚了，远比你罗列五六个要来得好。
范文
讲述自己参加国际数学奥林匹克的经历，以及对数学始终不变的热爱。
The moment I took the medal from Professor Zhexuan Tao, my heart was flying. Among all the contemporary mathematicians, Tao is undoubtedly an outstanding one—a Fields Medalist, and he has long been my idol. The first time I met him was on the podium of the 50th International Mathematic Olympic, where I was ranked forth. So far it has been the most brilliant accomplishment of mine in this field and one of those precious moments I will never forget. I have to say it is the result of both my talent and the unstoppable passion for math.
12岁开始，我就下决心一生都从事和追求数学。我对数字非常敏感。5岁时我就可以在一秒内心算九位数加、减、乘、除，并且总结了这些算法的内在规律。同年跟大学生一起参加计算比赛，最终打败所有的选手，取得了一级证书。
Since I was 12, I've decided that math will be my lifelong career and pursuit. I have been extremely sensitive to numbers since I was a little kid. At 5 years old, I could already mentally compute the addition, subtraction, multiplication and division of 9-digit numbers in one second, and I also concluded the internal regularity of these operations. The same year I joined in a national competition on computation with the undergraduates as my counterparts. During the process, I computed mentally while the others used the abacus. In the end, I beat them and got the national first-level certificate. The whole audience was astonished.
我数学学习的另外一个重要阶段就是被保送北京大学以后。这个时期的学习更加系统化，在这个过程中我学到的最有价值的经验就是对自己所钟爱的事业要有责任感。这种责任感促使我大量阅读来保持思维敏捷。
Another important stage for my math study is after being recommended for admission in the Beijing University. Study at this time is more systematic and of higher level, and through the study process, the most valuable experience I've learned is the sense of responsibility for the career I love, and this responsibility has driven me to read a lot to keep my thinking active. The deeper I get into this field, the more glamour I find about it. Later in my life, as I grow up and accumulate more through relevant books on the subject, I get to know math better and deeper. All the books I've read have been the best teachers ever, they not only teach me the knowledge, but more importantly, the way to think and to solve the problems.
在这儿我必须提到我母亲，是她引导我进入充满闪光数字的神奇世界，她是我的第一个导师和最好的支持者。在曾经的一次全国奥林比克训练项目中，面对来自各地的顶尖选手，我第一次感到自卑。是母亲不断鼓励我，让我有继续向前的勇气。
Here, I have to mention my mother, who guides me to the mysterious world with those glittering numbers and the labyrinth of the mixture behind them. She is my first mentor and my best supporter. Once upon a time in the national Olympic training campus, facing so many top students from the big cities, I felt inferior the first time that I was only another regular kid from nowhere on the road chasing his math dream. My mom encouraged me, and my enthusiasm kept me going. Through all these competitions, I've become stronger, more mature, and firmer than ever about the decision I made at 12.
最后我想说，尽管我是纯粹的科学信徒，我依然愿意认为数学就是我的命运，我对数学的爱好也是流淌在血液里的。不管前方有多少困难，我都会在这条路上走得更远。
At last, I want to say that though I am a pure scientific believer, I am willing to think math as my fate, and my interest in it has been in my blood. I will go farther in this road, no matter what and how many difficulties ahead.
分析
此篇文章作者讲述了他的三个小故事。第一个故事是参加国际数学奥林匹克，并获得第四名的成绩，这是作者一辈子难忘的回忆。第二个故事是作者五岁时跟大学生一起参加计算比赛，当别人用算盘的时候他心算就得出结果，最后获得一级证书，全场观众掌声如鸣。第三个小故事是进入北京大学进行系统的学习。最后，作者感谢自己的母亲，是她把作者带入数学的殿堂，并给予指引与帮助，以至于它已融入自己的血液中，成为自己的心头之爱。
这篇文章其实写得中规中矩，文章的极个别地方有点过于夸大的嫌疑。尽管如此，我们还是能清楚地看出作者对数学的喜爱之情以及在其中付出的心血与努力。也许这就是MIT最终录取他的原因。
